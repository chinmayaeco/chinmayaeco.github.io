Long-Run Production Cases

Real-world applications of production optimization

Tech Manufacturing

A smartphone factory can substitute robots for workers, but only to a point. The optimal combination depends on relative costs and desired output flexibility.

Cobb-Douglas α=0.7, β=0.3
Construction

Home builders face fixed proportions (Leontief) - each house needs 1 foundation, 4 walls, and 1 roof regardless of scale.

Leontief K/L = 2:1
Food Processing

A bakery can substitute between automated mixers and skilled bakers, but with diminishing marginal rates of technical substitution.

CES ρ=0.5
Logistics

Delivery services balance between drivers and vehicles, with nearly constant returns to scale when expanding operations.

Cobb-Douglas α=0.5, β=0.5

Strategic Production Decisions

Business applications of long-run production theory

Input Substitution

Key considerations when changing input ratios:

  • Monitor Marginal Rate of Technical Substitution (MRTS)
  • Adjust gradually to maintain quality
  • Consider training costs for new technologies
Expansion Planning

How returns to scale affect growth:

  • Increasing: Favorable for rapid expansion
  • Constant: Linear scaling works
  • Decreasing: Consider decentralization
Cost Minimization

Strategies for optimal input combinations:

  • Regularly reevaluate input prices
  • Automate when wage/rental ratio increases
  • Maintain flexibility in production processes
Risk Management

Diversifying production approaches:

  • Maintain multiple input source options
  • Balance capital and labor intensity
  • Monitor technological changes that affect MRTS
