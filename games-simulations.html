Climate Policy Approaches

Two main approaches to climate policy:

  • Market Solutions: Use price signals and economic incentives (carbon taxes, cap-and-trade, subsidies)
  • Non-Market Solutions: Use regulations, standards, and direct government action (emissions standards, renewable mandates, public investment)

Effective climate policy often combines both approaches.

Case Study: Climate Policy in Practice

Scenario: Achieving Net Zero by 2050

Your economy needs to transform while maintaining growth:

  • Current emissions trajectory leads to 3°C warming
  • Energy system heavily dependent on fossil fuels
  • Significant economic costs of climate inaction
  • Opportunities in green technology and innovation

Your Challenge: Design policies to decarbonize the economy while maintaining prosperity.

Real-World Policy Examples

Market Solutions in Practice

  • European Emissions Trading System: Largest carbon market, covers 45% of EU emissions
  • British Columbia Carbon Tax: Revenue-neutral tax with proven emissions reductions
  • Renewable Energy Tax Credits: Accelerated solar and wind deployment in US

Non-Market Solutions in Practice

  • EU Vehicle Emissions Standards: Mandated progressive reduction in fleet emissions
  • China's Renewable Mandates: Required utilities to source specific renewable percentages
  • California Building Codes: Progressive energy efficiency requirements

Economic Impacts of Climate Policy

Well-designed climate policy can have multiple benefits:

  • Short-term GDP impact typically 1-2% by 2050 for 2°C pathways
  • Co-benefits include reduced air pollution, energy security, innovation
  • Costs of inaction far exceed costs of action (2-20% of GDP by 2100)
  • Just transition policies critical for political feasibility

Climate Risk Management

Businesses face two types of climate risk:

  • Physical Risks: Direct impacts of climate change (extreme weather, supply chain disruption)
  • Transition Risks: Policy, technology, and market changes during low-carbon transition

Forward-looking companies are integrating climate into core strategy.
